Let there be the vowel इ in the room of the vowel अ coming before the letter क coming in an affix, when the feminine affix आप् follows — provided that the feminine affix आप् does not come AFTER a case-affix. Thtts, [from sarvaka "every"] सर्विका, [from कारक "a maker"] कारिका.

Why do we say "of the vowel अ"? Witness naukA "boat."

Why do we say "coming in a affix"?
Witness zakA [from zaka] "who is able— [where the k belongs to the verbal root].

Why do we say "not after a case-affix"?
Witness bahuparivrAjakA nagarI "a city with many religious, mendicants"— [where the feminine affix is attached after the case-affix had been elided as explained under in 964, informing the compound]. Here bahuparivrAjaka becomes bahuparivrAjakA becomes of an adjective of bahuvrihi samasa to describe the city
